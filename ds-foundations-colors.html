Primary color

A primary color is the color displayed most frequently on our screens and components.

To create contrast between elements, you can use lighter or darker tones of your primary color. The contrast between lighter and darker tones helps show division between surfaces, such as between the status bar and a toolbar.

Support Color

The secondary color is used to accent select parts of our UI. It should contrast with elements that surround it and be applied sparingly as an accent.

While black is our primary support color it is never reccomended to use pure black. For most components we use S-Light #2c2c2c or 80% black.

Accent colors

Accent colors are used to draw the users attention to a particular action of element. They should be used sparingly and with great care.

The primary use for accent colors are:

  • Buttons, floating action buttons, and button text
  • Text fields, cursors, and text selection
  • Progress bars
  • Selection controls, buttons, and sliders
  • Links
  • Headlines

Disabled Button

Used primarily to indicate that additional information is needed.

Style

Every button for Allied Electronics can be built following the following criteria.

Maintain a 2pd border radius on all Allie Electronics Buttons. Minimum 36pd hieght with 16pd left and right margin and padding for every button. We use our brand font Verdana in ALL CAPS minimum size of 12pd.

Adding an Icon
When adding an icon to a button maitain a 6px padding between the verbiage and the icon. Continue to maintain the min height and padding to the left and right.
